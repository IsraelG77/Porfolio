About

Upgrading how organizations make People decisions

From Psychology to HR Decision Architecture: a trajectory built upon criteria, operations, and technology.

My corporate experience revealed a recurring pattern: critical decisions made with high conviction but low structure — implicit criteria, limited traceability, poor system integration, and hidden costs for both candidates and the organization.

Throughout my background in Tech Recruitment, People Ops, HR Systems, and People Analytics, I have navigated within these processes while maintaining a critical perspective—executing daily routines while questioning how decisions were formulated, justified, and operationalized.

Today, I work to upgrade these processes by combining data analytics, workflow design, automation, and behavioral diagnosis applied to corporate operations. My true objective is to act as a Decision Architect, driving clarity and consistency in people-related definitions.

Value Proposition

My true differentiator isn't just visualization. It's the structural foundation that makes People decisions explicit and trustworthy.

Dashboards are just the final interface. The actual value lies in translating business problems, criteria, data, and operations into a usable decision layer.

Understanding People problems from within the operation
My background in psychology combined with hands-on experience in recruiting, People Ops, and BP allows me to identify exactly where decisions lose quality: fragile criteria, poorly defined stages, cross-functional friction, and low traceability.
